## The name is the license

An encompassing "above" registration should be knowable **by its type, in the language
it expresses it** — so that anything meeting us by behavior can read our sense of
licensing and consentful use *from the title itself.* Our license has to occupy the
title.

**Antidote** does that. It is the join of *anti(body)* + *(anec)dote* — self-consistent
with the metaphors we already carry — and it encodes the reason for being: an **antidote
to a known class of abuse** — the piracy and forgery of consent, and the taking of
consent for financial gain. The name is the mission statement.

### It names the "place above" (#88) without becoming an operator

Antidote answers #88's "this needs to be identified": the place above now has a name.
It stays a **position, not an operating apex.** Antidote-the-501(c)(4) is its **first
occupant** — it operates only *its own* branch (its own contracts, donations, sales) —
never an enforced headquarters over the network. **FCCN-ANTIBODY sits beneath Antidote**
in the org chart; taking Antidote's board takes only what Antidote itself ran. Same
anti-capture line `NONPROFIT.draft-0.md` drew, now named.

## The mission, finally in words

**Read the metadata, and act — to fix stolen consent.** That is the 501(c)(4)'s
wildcard mission in a nutshell. The constitutions in this ecosystem are strongly worded
about one thing above all: **proof of consent** — including how to use consent to *drive
background processes*, slowly, at our own pace.

The mission rests on a thesis (recorded as motivation, for counsel to weigh — not a legal
assertion): consent is routinely pirated and forged, remedy is owed, and someone suited
to *recognize the signal and build the fix* can pursue it — eventually even without the
counterparties' cooperation.

## Proof of consent, at our own pace — the queue

We can say, from now on, what everyone else says: **We Are Experiencing Higher Busyness
Than Usual.** We can make you wait however long it takes for us to *see and consent* to
a thing. **There is a queue** — human-paced, consent-gated. This is the built consent
ladder (ambient / confirmed / standing; the Elevated holds) turned into a stance: nothing
proceeds until it has been seen and consented to, and that pace is a feature, not an
apology.

## Sheltering, not harvesting

The prevailing data-broker and privacy-registry model asks you to hand over the very data
you want protected — you give the owner/operator all of it to keep it from being stolen.
Antidote inverts that. You keep your data; you are pseudonymous; **we read the *metadata*
and act on it.** The model shelters you rather than collecting you.

- **Met-receipts are the evidence ledger.** On Antidote you can see the **met-receipt for
  every transfer you have ever made from here** (`met.mjs`). Because we can show you what
  a present receipt looks like, we know what a **missing** receipt looks like — and a
  missing receipt is the tell of stolen consent, and the basis to pursue it for you. This
  is the enforcement substrate that complements `NONPROFIT.draft-0.md`'s "provable
  lineage."
- **The consent-breach notice feed.** When a dataset is known to have been sold, we can
  post signed **notices that Atlases check-fetch and Tells check.** No new crypto: a
  breach notice is a first-party, signed *claim about a subject* (civic-node
  OPEN-QUESTIONS §N — "claims-about-a-subject need no new primitive"), kin to a quell,
  pointing at a dataset instead of a poll.

## Adapter-based, forkable, defined by signals

We are **one implementation** of something that can look very different in other applied
domains but **produces the same signals in artifacts.** Everything specific to us is
"whatever-to-cope" detail of our implementation. So we intend to be **adapter-based from
the start** — able to plug into anything of any shape that we can **first-contact meet
and judge for ourselves** whether it works — and to keep **multiple forks** available, so
the terms can be inspected and the TOS proven **unfettered.**

> **North star (unspecced):** the portable thing is the *proof-of-consent signal
> conformance* — the signals an artifact must carry — held separate from the antibody
> implementation. That conformance is what a fork proves it honors.

## Money, safely — at a layer to be decided

Antidote takes **donations** and can process **sales verified within our own platform**.
Our appearance is load-bearing here: a nonprofit can safely move money **because the money
moves under a new consent document — *our constitutions* — not under everyone else's terms
of service, which we have never read.**

- **On anecdote:** a payment platform for sales — e.g. pictures sold as tickets for entry
  and later redeemed — under anecdote's own CONSTITUTION, which forbids data monitoring.
- **App-store presence:** a branded, app-store-fit Antidote presence to streamline a
  **replacement for the Elevated frame** while we run from service-worker-takeover risk
  (couples the "bind-the-queen" / dumb-shell concern), and to process in-platform-verified
  sales.
- **Business shape:** pay data-originators for consentful data, and contract with
  government to buy it **legitimately** rather than let it be scalped by brokers — an org
  pulling money *toward* the people whose consent it is.

> **OPEN QUESTION — the payment layer.** At what layer money lives is not decided:
> Antidote-org for donations and government contracts; anecdote-level for sales and
> tickets under its no-monitoring constitution. To be resolved deliberately, not by
> default.

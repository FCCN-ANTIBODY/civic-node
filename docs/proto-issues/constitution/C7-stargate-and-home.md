## Context a cold agent needs (do not re-derive)

- **Verbatim:** survive a collapsing universe by **moving out** — relocate the **worker, the troves,
  and the maintenance tasks** to a new home. **Castling** (swap the online repo for the local one)
  generalized to **egress across mediums or to a new domain.** Doomed to cat-and-mouse, **evict our
  storage and take it elsewhere.** Keep a **stargate ready before we know what to connect it to**, and
  the **trust test for a new home written before the options appear** (a home you launch yourself is up
  as long as the callsign is, anyway). The **HOME document**: extract the home-shaped concerns out of
  your constitution into a passive manifest of **what home looks like**, unused until ready to send,
  **signed by itself, through the stargate.** Relocation is **first contact in reverse — verifiable
  self-dehydration to a QR that matches the one you arrived on** — our lessons the **proof of what a
  better home would have to mean.**
- **What exists:** `git-enough.md` (**Castle** = full-lineage import, **King's Leap** = photocopy-as-
  fresh-root; the offline origin **publishes** — pushes downstream, send-pack built); #71 (signed
  snapshot export — the HOME manifest is snapshot-shaped: sign the content of a checkout);
  `transfer.mjs packLayout` (attest a whole set — self-dehydration); origin.md first-contact
  signer-pinning (the QR the HOME must match).

## Do
- [ ] The stargate: an egress abstraction with adapters (a medium; a new domain) that moves worker +
      troves + maintenance out — Castling generalized past git-into-git.
- [ ] The trust test for a new home: written now, applied to candidates as they appear (what a home
      must prove before you ship to it).
- [ ] The HOME document: a passive, self-signed manifest of what home looks like, extracted from the
      constitution, unused until ship-out; verify it dehydrates to a QR matching first-contact.
- [ ] Tests: a HOME doc round-trips (dehydrate → QR → rehydrate) and self-verifies; a candidate home
      failing the trust test is refused before any trove ships.

## Constraints
Signed-by-itself (the HOME doc is its own root — first contact in reverse). No trove ships to an
untested home. Reuse #71's content-signing + git-enough's send-pack — don't invent a second export.
The callsign is optional (§S). Show intent verifiably (aliens).
